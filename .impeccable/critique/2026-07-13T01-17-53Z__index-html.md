## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Not AI slop. Committed, specific identity (neon cob, plate chip, instrument-cluster gauge) executed with real craft. One absolute-ban hit: **glassmorphism-as-default** — `.panel` (blur + translucency + glass edge) is the universal surface for every section on every page, plus the blurred quickbar. Thematically motivated, but glass is the default rather than a moment. Hero-metric structure on costs.html is benign (it IS the tool's output). A suppressed `.panel-title .num` rule suggests numbered scaffolding was added then hidden — dead CSS to remove. No side-stripes, no gradient text, no text overflow at 375/1280.

**Deterministic scan:** 14 findings, all one rule — **em-dash-overuse** in copy, on 14 of 15 pages (404.html clean). Partial false positives: the footer tagline repeats one em-dash on every page, index's "—" is a UI placeholder, about.html uses them as list separators. But the core signal is real: the body copy leans on em-dashes as sentence glue (the writing tell of its AI author). In-page DOM detector: **zero anti-patterns** on index, costs, e30-blend (injection succeeded on a localhost copy; production CSP would have blocked it — working as designed).

**Where they agree/disagree:** The detector confirms the DOM/visual layer is clean — the issues worth fixing are judgment-level (density, hierarchy, state integrity), which only Assessment A caught. The detector caught the copy cadence that A (same author) was blind to.

## Priority Issues

1. **[P1] The OBD block outranks the core task.** Two neon buttons, a 60-word adapter-jargon paragraph, and an affiliate link sit ABOVE the first required input (TANK SIZE). First-timers hit hardware talk + commerce before the tool asks its first question. Fix: collapse into a `<details>` styled like the tester ("READ FROM CAR — OBD-II helper"), or move below TARGET BLEND.
2. **[P1] SETUP is 9 flat chunks and buries the steering wheel.** Vehicle ID, Bluetooth, fuel state, pump properties, and the goal all share one panel with identical styling; TARGET BLEND — the tool's central decision — is visually last-and-equal. Fix: three labeled groups (YOUR TANK / THE PUMPS / TARGET) and give the target real prominence.
3. **[P2] Warnings never reach the point of action on mobile.** `#status-msg` lives in THE MIX; the quickbar mirrors only numbers. Ask for E98 with a half tank of E10 and the quickbar shows "E66" with no cue why. Fix: warn state on the quickbar (amber tint + indicator when status ≠ ok).
4. **[P2] Contradictory command at the most dangerous moment.** In `bad_mix`, the warning says the fuels can't steer the blend while ADD PUMP GAS still glows "12.00 gal". Fix: dim or dash the order cards for bad_mix/tank_full; keep values for target_above/below where they're genuinely actionable.
5. **[P3] Display font + sub-11px uppercase in interactive controls.** Righteous at ~10.9px in buttons/nav, badges ~10px. Keep Righteous for readouts (gauge, order values — instrument cluster earns it); move buttons/nav to Outfit 600 ≥12px.

## Persona Red Flags

**Alex (power user):** silent localStorage restore — last fill's fuel level pre-filled with no "restored" cue; OBD prose in his editing path; no typed input for target; tank accepts 999 silently.
**Jordan (first-timer):** three ethanol controls + target to disambiguate; two identical vehicle pickers one page apart (which one do I use?); vehicle picker covers only 2005+ curated models with no guidance when a car's absent; adapter word salad (Veepeak/vLinker/OBDLink/ELM327) reads as gatekeeping.
**Casey (mobile, one-handed):** everything hangs on the quickbar, which carries no warning state; nav links are 111×30px (under 44px); full-width 44px-tall sliders in the scroll path invite accidental drags while thumb-scrolling.

## Minor Observations

- costs.html break-even number stays green even when the verdict is "you're paying more per mile" — semantic color mismatch (arguably P2).
- Identical gradient underline under every panel title on every page — a repeated tic; one deliberate accent would be stronger.
- Fine-print duplicates the gauge readout verbatim.
- Affiliate tag is still `dosecomp-20` (cross-property; known TODO).
- Mix-bar colors must be learned; labels save the order cards.
- `.gauge-label` at 10.6px/.34em tracking under the hero number is at the legibility edge.
- Genuinely good: complete focus-visible coverage, handled empty states, ~7:1 dim-text contrast, zero consistency drift across 15 pages.

## Questions to Consider

1. If the defaults already produce a valid answer, why does mobile lead with 2,200px of inputs? What would "answer first, refine below" look like?
2. Should CAN IT RUN E85? merge into the SETUP vehicle fieldset — one car entry answering tank size AND compatibility AND a sane target ceiling?
3. The gauge shows the blend you chose; the gallons are what you act on at the pump. Is the visual apex pointing at the wrong number?
